This is my understanding of why SOP (Statement of Purpose/research statement) after certain research and their references

### Variation's in SOP and its jargon

- Based on country and university SOP can have different approaches
    - In US universities its called Statement of Purpose (SOP) / research statement / personal statements
    - In UK universities its called research proposal / SOP / research statement/ they have explict questions to answer

- Difference between SOP and personal statement
    - If explictly asked for personal statement, it is just SOP
        - Personal statement can have more personal touch, like why you want to do PhD, your journey etc. But keep it max professional. less of the personal stories.
    - if asked SOP with personal statement, then there is no confusion.

- Keep it plain with no fancy formatting unless specifically asked for.
    -  Times New Roman, 12-point font, double-spaced, 1-inch margins is standard unless otherwise specified.

### why SOP?

First of all, the name itself has a lot to tell you about it, Statement of Purpose. PhD is a long journey and you need a very purpose to not to get lost in anyfield vastness. 

Think this way, what would you look if you are "Hiring a Student who will work with you for next 5-6 years?" keep in mind this person can influence your research and lab environment.

1. I would look for someone who has a clear purpose of why he/she wants to do PhD? and goal in his/her mind. {**Question**}

2. I would look for someone who has a background in the field and has done some research in the field. {**Background/skills/leastpossible training required**}

3. Finally what he/she knows about my research and what he/she likes about it. {**Interest in my research and did some homework**}

Its like a sales pitch, you are selling yourself to the professor and convince him/her that you are the right person for the job. its worth his/her time and effort to invest in you. Remember its a huge pain to get funding for you and teach you how to do research while managing other students and research. Unfortunately, this how system works.

### How to write SOP?

1. You need to show why you are the right person for the job. (if you project is already defined like in EU PhD programs.). Show that you have clear idea on question you wanted to work on.

2. Highlight what you got in your scientific arsenal to do the job. (your background, skills, research experience etc.) And how you are going to use them.

3. Show that you have done your homework on the professor research (You know what he or she has been working on) and why you are interested in working with him/her. (mention specific papers, projects etc.)

###

1. A personal statement must make it clear why that applicant wants to pursue a PhD in neuroscience specifically. 
    - A broad flowery description about the applicant’s interest in biology since they were 5 years old is not helpful.
    - This statement is easier if the applicant has some laboratory research experience and can speak to why that research experience was motivating. A clear articulation of “why neuroscience” is imperative.

2. Your research in most important aspect of this statement. Thus, provide a description of the independent research you have done till date somewhere in the application. 
    - The research description should focus on the big picture: 
        - What was the big question? 
        - What choices were made in the experiments?
        - What controls were done?
        - Why were the specific controls used? 
    - The applicant should do this for each distinct research project. 
    - This shows the admissions committee how the applicant thinks about science; understanding the process is more important than if there were positive results.
    - Conclusion and if the applicant has publications, preprints, or posters, these could be mentioned in this section.

3. why you are applying to the particular program.
    - A good way to show that the applicant has spent time looking at the specific graduate program and has thought about which programs were a good fit for their interests is by identifying programmatic strengths, 
    - Examples:
        - the expertise of the faculty 
        - identifying other specific or unique aspects that differentiate the program, such as, for example, our Itasca program [see below].

4. Please proofread your statements. 
    - Typographic errors
    - poor grammaran 
    - and other sloppy writing
    - Theses mistakes you did not take the time or effort to ensure quality. 
    - It may seem silly to mention, but it is important to make sure that when mentioning programmatic strengths, the applicant should be sure that these are the programmatic strengths of the institution to which the application is sent.
    - No Generative AI usage
    - Get it reviewed by your peers/mentors
    - Avoid cliches
    - Don't repeat your CV 
    - Don't find and replace text like "University X" with actual university name. tailor it for each university.
